Japanese Curry

Description

Japanese Curry is a quick and easy way to bring together multiple hearty ingredients into a tasty dish. The roots of this recipe come from the Japan during the Meji Period(1868-1912) when the ingredients were first introduced to them by the british.

This recipe can come many differe forms from Pork, Beef, Lamb, Chicken, Lentil, and even Shrimp. But the ingredients still remain mostly the same unless the cook would like to add more vegatables. Once you are good at this recipes trying adding new things to the mix to see if it blends well.

Ingredients

  • 1/5 lbs bonesless, skinless chicken thighs
  • 1/4 tsp kosher salt
  • freshly ground black pepper
  • 2 carrots
  • 2 onions
  • 3 yukon gold potatoes
  • 1 knob of ginger
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • 1/2 apple
  • 1.5 Tbsp neutral-flavored oil
  • 4 cups Chicken Stock/broth
  • 1 Tbsp honey
  • 1 Tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 Tbsp ketchup
  • 1 package Japanese Curry Roux
  • 2-4 tsp Kosher salt
  • 8 servings cooked rice
